# Brand Strategy

## Positioning

**Concilium occupies the "deliberation" territory in dev tooling.**

Competitors brand on speed, flow, or automation (Linear, Jira, Devin, Cursor,
Copilot). Concilium's differentiator is not "AI builds software" — everyone
claims that. It is **structured deliberation**: every ticket has seats, every
seat has a voice (human or AI stand-in), and nothing ships without consensus.

The brand story is the *legitimacy of the build*: every perspective was heard,
agreement was reached, and the shipped artifact has provenance. The name
"Concilium" (Latin: council, assembly) carries the metaphor natively and gives
us a deep vocabulary well — seats, quorum, the Mediator, verdicts.

### Positioning statement

> For product teams adopting AI-driven development, Concilium is the workflow
> tool where software is **decided, then built**. Unlike single-agent coding
> tools, Concilium convenes a council — human stakeholders and AI stand-ins in
> named seats — and only builds what the council agrees on.

### Positioning options considered

1. **"The Council"** *(chosen)* — software decided, not just generated.
   Weighty but warm, slightly ceremonial, self-aware enough not to be stuffy.
2. **"Never build alone"** — collaboration-first, multiplayer/presence
   emphasis. Rejected: drifts into Figma/Notion territory we can't win on.
3. **"AI with checks and balances"** — trust/governance framing. Rejected as
   primary: brands us as a safety product rather than a building product.
   The council framing *implies* both 2 and 3.

## Brand pillars

1. **Every seat is filled.** No perspective goes missing; an AI stand-in holds
   the chair until a human takes it. This is the most marketable mechanic —
   name it and own it.
2. **Consensus before code.** The build is the *output* of agreement, not a
   hopeful prompt.
3. **The Mediator, not the manager.** AI facilitates human judgment; it
   doesn't replace it.
4. **Provenance.** Every shipped artifact traces back to who approved it
   and why.

## Audience

- **Primary:** small product teams (2–15 people) adopting agentic coding who
  are uneasy about unreviewed AI output — they want the speed without losing
  the cross-functional check.
- **Secondary:** solo builders who want the *discipline* of a full team —
  the AI stand-ins give one person an Engineer, Designer, PO, and QA.

## Taglines

| Tagline | Use |
| --- | --- |
| **"Software by consensus."** | Primary. Short, ownable, says the whole thing. |
| **"Give every project a council."** | Landing-page hero (Claude Design system). |
| "You steer; they do the legwork, in concert." | Hero subhead / supporting line. |
| "Every seat filled. Every voice heard. Then it builds." | Long-form alt hero. |
| "Where code gets a council." | Secondary / social. |
| "Decided, then built." | Secondary / footer, decks. |
| "The ticket is alive." | Dev-audience campaigns; leans on the living-ticket concept. Not the masthead. |
| "AI holds the seat until you take it." | Feature-level, for stand-ins specifically. |

**Avoid:** "supercharge", "10x", "copilot", "agentic", rocket emoji. That is
the crowded register we differentiate *against*.
